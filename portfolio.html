Countries I've traveled to in no particular order

ITALY

Visiting Italy was a dream come true, it was such an enchanting experience that left a mark on my heart. The journey began in Rome, where history seemed to seem from every corner. Walking through the ancient ruins of the Colosseum and the Roman Forum was like stepping back in time. The grandeur of the Vatican City, with the Sistine Chapel adorned with the Michelangelo's masterpieces was absolutely breathtaking. Each street and piazza had it's own charm, and I savored the rich flavors of Italian cuisine, from creamy gelato to savory pasta dishes.

After Rome, I headed to Florence. In Florence, I was priviledged enough to go on a truffle hunting tour. Afterwards, we actually got to eat the truffles we found. Let's just say - truffle is not as luxurious as they make it out to be in the US. I then headed to the islands, Naples, Positano, and Capri. What really surprised me was transportation in these islands are limited. Therefore, do not rent accomdation at the top of the island because you'll end up having to hike every time you go out.

Korea

I absolutely love Korean culture. I love their food, music, mannerisms, and just so much more. The country's history is preserved in its ancient palaces, traditional Hanok villages, and beautiful temples, offering a glimpse into its past. Walking through the streets of Seoul, there are bustling markets such as Namdaemun and Gwangjang. Their deep respect for tradition and balance with contemporary advancements make this country a facinating place to explore.

On top of that, the Korea wave has deeply influenced my affection for the country. The global popularity of k-pop, k-dramas and korean cinema really showcases Korea's creative prowess and cultural influence. Groups like BTS and Blackpink, along with great dramas like Crash Landing On You have really brought Koreans culture to the forefront of entertainment.

Spain

I love spain for its diverse landscapes. The historic cities like Madrid, Barcelona and Seville and brimming with architectural wonders. In Madrid, the grandeur of the Royal Palace and the masterpieces housed in the Prado Museum captivate the senses. Barcelona enchants with the iconic Sagrada Familiar and Park Guell, while Sevilles vibrant flameco performances and the beautiful Alcazar really transports visitors to a different era.

The culinary delights, from apas and paellas to regional specialities like jamon iberico and churros, reflects the diverse influenxes that define Spanish cuisine. Also the warm hospitality, passionate music and dance and the very relaxed yet vibrant lifestyle that creates an extremely refreshing atmosphere for someone like me who grew up in the bustling city like New York. I cannot wait to go back.
